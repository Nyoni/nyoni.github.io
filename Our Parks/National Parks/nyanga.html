Gonarezhou National Park

The Park covers 5 000km2

Woodlands – including alluvial, mopane, miombo, combretum, dry forests and wooded grasslands.
The Park also possess baobab trees.

Gonarezhou hosts 89 larger and 61 smaller mammal species, 400 bird species and 50 fish species (including Zambezi shark and small-tooth sawfish at the confluence of the Runde and Save rivers).Home to elephant, buffalo, lion and leopard.

April – Wet season comes to an end.
Temperatures begin to cool especially at night.
May, June, July & August – Winter period and coldest time, with August warming a bit.
Day temperatures are around 270C/810F.
Night time temperatures are around 120C/540F.
September & October – Days are hot with over 310C/880F.
November –Wet season begin.
December, January and February –Wettest period.
Daytime temperature averages 320C/900F.
Nighttime and early morning average 200C/680F.
March – Rains diminish. Temperatures average between 310C/880F and 190C/660F during day and night.

How to get there
Access to Gonarezhou is by road and air.

Access by road
Chipanda Pools access ( Runde and Save sub-regions)
Follow main tarred road from the Chiredzi turn-off to Mutare to Mutare for 21km.
Turn off to the south at the Chipinda Pools sign post.
Follow the gravel road for approximately 34km to the entrance of the Park, about 55km from Chiredzi.

Travel Tips
Carry food supplies enough for the stay.
Carry Medical Aid Kit
Carry malaria and insect repellant.
Mosquito nets
