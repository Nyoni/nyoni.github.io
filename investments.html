Investments

Investing in wildlife Whats there to invest? How to invest in wildlife? Types of investments

The sustenance of the environment requires financial resources. Whats more crucial, however, is to have more hands on deck to attend to the many situations that characterize wildlife management.

Zimparks employs a science based approach to wildlife utilization by ensuring that the backbone of all park management plans is the ecological programme facilitated by two departments that specialize in Aquatic and Terrestial Research manned by qualified and well experienced scientists.

Investment in the Parks estate, not only directly fund the preservation of the habitats therein, but it spreads the gospel of conservation to the rest of the world by educating tourists who in some cases even opt to be part of the conservation community.

It has come to pass that concessions located in some remote part of parks contribute immensely towards antipoaching through deterring incursions by human presence. The roads constructed for tourism service delivery are also used to rapidly deploy antipoaching reaction teams.

Wildlife Investments also enhance game water supply and also spreads benefits to communities through proffering employment opportunities and direct income from village tours or game meat.

The additional eyes assist in spotting signs of incursions as well as animals in distress and ecological data such as animal sightings.

This is what defines sustainable utilization of wildlife in Zimbabwe: economic and ecologically beneficial investments that contribute towards the capacity of the current generation to hand over a healthy environment to the future generation.

The Authority shall periodically publish available investment opportunities that arise, however, the Authority is amenable to engaging investors that have ideas of their own.

The Spectrum of Wildlife Investments in the Parks Estate spans from visitor activities that have short term (annual) permits, seasonal safari camps that have medium term (5 Years) tenures and major investments with tenures beyond 5 years. (Picture of a Boat Cruise) Picture of a TEC Picture of Nyamatusi Camp in Mana Pools.

Joint Ventures with Zimparks for new major construction projects in designated Development Areas, that is, wherein permanent structures are permitted exclusively, are possible with both local and foreign investors in line with the provisions of the Zimbabwe Investment Development Agency Act, Part III which outlines types of public private partnerships.

All investors in the Parks Estate are required to comply with all regulations related to the respective line of business which Zimparks shall assist with guidance and support letters where necessary.
Point of Note. . . .

Environmental and Social Impact Assessments

  • All major investments will conduct an Environmental and Social Impact Assessment (ESIA) which will include public participation.
  • The investor is responsible for costing, funding and conducting the ESIA and submitting results to Zimparks for consideration. The ESIA guides the magnitude /possibility of developments for a specific site or area.
  • Permit level activities do not necessarily require an EIA as the carrying capacity of such activities would have been determined at Park Planning stage through scientific research.

Monitoring and Evaluation

  • Sustainable utilisation requires constant monitoring and evaluation to ensure projects do not develop negative impacts to the environment after inception.
  • Routine monitoring visits to the partnership/lease for evaluation provides evidence that the objectives of the project are being met or otherwise.
  • Systems are in place for monitoring and evaluation of investments include analysis of returns on investments, performance measurements, conservation support, community benefits and other various areas.
  • The contracts for major projects shall include provisions for mandatory submission of audited accounts annually.

All investments are subject to permit or lease fees. Permit fees are published by the Authority by Gazette or on the website.

Short Term Investments

These are usually tourist activities or consumptive businesses that are characterized by seasonal demand or cycles. Each park has activities permissible depending on its natural resource endowment.

Zimparks offers investment opportunities for consumptive and non-consumptive operations. Consumptive businesses such as fishing, have a maximum number of permits as the environment can only sustain a limited number. Every proposed activity is subjected to an ecological assessment based on the proposed location to establish whether there are intolerable impacts the activity may have on the local environment. There is no guarantee that permits shall be issued therefore.

The Parks Estate is vast. It is crucial to be definitive on the location of the project to enable the Authority to conduct an ecological assessment or an Environmental and Social Impact Assessment. The first point of call is therefore to visit the specific park and engage the Park Management to establish permissible investments, options of locations for the proposed investment and any information that enables investors to draft a business plan.

The investor can thereafter submit a formal expression of interest to establish a business within the parks estate specifying the nature of business and the respective protected area.

Short and Medium Term Investments Procedure

Photographic Tourism and other Permits: Apply for a permit in writing to the Director General on email patodirectorgeneral@zimparks.org.zw

Temporary Extended Camps

Temporary extended camps are issued in line with sustainable tourism philosophy that promote minimum impact to the environment. These camps have no exclusive zones and there is no infrastructure development on site. This type of lease is issued for a maximum of 5 years and renewed annually.

Hunting Concessions

Investors may acquire a hunting concession through an Auction or tender process. The tenure for a Hunting concession is 5 years renewable to a maximum of 10 years.
New concepts are considered for a one year trial period during which time ecological research is conducted on their impact to the environment and possibility of adopting the concept for annual renewal.

Long Term Investment Models Proceedure

The long term investment models within the Parks and Wildlife Estate include Photographic Concessions and Joint Ventures that have a tenure of up to 25 years comprising of 5 year review/renewal periods.

Concessions – For Unsolicited Bids, submit an expression of interest for a concession to the Director General by email: patodirectorgeneral@zimparks.org.zw

Outright Lease

An investor is allocated operating rights over a defined location within a park and pays annual lease fees and is applicable consumptive and non-consumptive operations. Other regulatory fees such as conservation fees payable from the visitors shall apply.

Public Private Partnerships

A contractual agreement where a private investor performs an institution’s functions or makes use of state property for its own commercial purposes. The private party assumes substantial financial, technical and operational risks associated with those functions or the use of state property. In return, the private party receives a benefit according to pre-defined performance criteria.

Built Operate and Transfer

A private partner undertakes the financing and construction and the operation and maintenance thereof. The partner operates the facility over a fixed term, collecting all the revenue to enable recovery of the investment in the project. This applies to permanent infrastructure such as hotels. Other regulatory fees apply.

Disposal of Investment Opportunities /Standard Procedures

When applying for outright leasing rights or Public Private Partnership (PPP) arrangements with Zimparks, there are two processes available depending on the nature of the opportunity. Engagement of investors can be through competitive bidding processes or unsolicited bids as guided by the ZIDA Act.

Procedure for Unsolicited Bids

  • There are instances where an investor identifies the opportunity for a business within the Parks and Wildlife estate. These are unsolicited bids.
  • “unsolicited bid, or expression of interest” refer to a proposal that is prepared or made without the invitation, solicitation, supervision or request.
  • All unsolicited bids should be submitted in writing addressed to the Director General.
  • The Authority reserves the right to reject or accept a proposal classified as an Unsolicited bid.
  • The bidder shall be responsible for incurring the costs of any familiarisation or assessment visits or Environmental Impact Assessments required thereof.
