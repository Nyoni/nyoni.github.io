Licensing

  1. Examinations – theory, shooting, oral and practical

    The ZPWMA in conjunction with the Zimbabwe Professional Hunters and Guides Association (ZPHGA) offers.

  2. Learner pprofessional hunters’ licence (LPH)

  3. Professional hunters’ (PH)

  4. Professional guides’ (PG)

  5. River guide authority (RGA)

  6. Rafting guide licence


LPH examinations: this course consists of four compulsory modules which are Law; Habits and Habitats; Fire Arms and General Paper and one elective module (Canoeing). For one to qualify to sit for the LPH examinations, one is required to be -:


  • A Zimbabwean citizen or has a permanent residence status.
  • Be over 18 years.
  • Have a valid first aid certificate of your choice (MARS, EMRAS, St Johns, ACE, Red Cross).
  • Possess or has a Zimbabwe National Identity card / valid passport or drivers licence.

When the candidate passes three modules out of the four modules offered, they are given a second chance to write the failed subjects and if they pass all they are issued with a Learner Professional Hunter’s licence. Candidates who pass two subjects and below are considered to have failed and should rewrite all the four modules. The LPH licence is renewed yearly and is valid for a calendar year, therefore, it should be renewed yearly. When a student is renewing his or her license, they are asked to fill in a license application form, provide either a copy of their valid passport or national ID; tutorship letter from either a full PG or PH whom they will be under the attachment of. LPH renewal fee is US$50.00 per calendar year. The LPH student is allowed to go for shooting tests and attend oral interviews after at least two years on relevant experience in Zimbabwe as a student.


Requirements for Oral Interviews for Learner Professional Hunters / guides

  • Valid first Aid Certificate.
  • Shooting Certificate.
  • Valid learner professional hunters’ licence.
  • Two years relevant experience gained in Zimbabwe.
  • For a hunter; s / he should have shot at least five big dangerous game by self.
  • For a Guide s / he should have shot at least four big dangerous game by self.

Proficiency tests will then be conducted after at lest two years upon which the student has proven beyond reasonable doubts that s /he has gained the practical experience during attachment.

If a candidate obtains a professional hunters’ licence, they are authorised to do hunting and guiding and if s / he obtains a professional guides’ licence they are authorised to do guiding only. The PH and PG licences are renewed each year as they are valid for each calendar year only at US$200.00 and US$100.00 respectively by the licence holder.


  1. Personal effects permits

    These are finished products that are to be used personally by the client. The client should be registered as either a trophy dealers’ license or there should be proof of ownership of the finished products to be exported. The client is also required to provide proof that s/he is relocating.

    These finished products include belts, handbags, tanned skins ivory souvenirs, etc. Clients are required to complete an application form in order to be issued with a personal permit.

  2. Hunted trophies

    There is need for TRAS2 hunting return form. This permit is issued to clients who will have been authorized to hunt either a leopard, lion, elephant, buffalo, etc

  3. Export/ Import of specially protected plants

    The client is required to fill in an application form attach invoice from the receiving country. A client id required to be licensed with a permit to propagate.

  4. Export of live animals

    The client should be registered either as a holder of a breeders permit or permit to keep or registration of farm or concession or a wildlife farm in order to export or import. The property sending or receiving the animals should be assessed by the Ecologist. Leopard and elephant export permits need import permit from the receiving country.

General Permits

  1. Commercial kapenta fishing permits

    These are limited though oversubscribed.

  2. Capture and translocation permit; permit to sell; permit to donate live animals.

    This permit is issued to those clients either registered as breeders of animals or clients with a permit to keep.

    Requirements

    • Formal applications from both the sending and the receiving property.
    • Both properties should be assessed before the permit is issued.
    • They should also attach valid permits, for example, to keep wildlife, wildlife ranching, breeders.
    • A payment of non-refundable fee of US$100.00 is paid by the person who is sending whilst a fixed amount of US$200.00 is paid by the person receiving the animals.
    • 2% of the value of the animals is required to be paid by the person who will be capturing the animals.

  3. Commercial fishing in private dams (RDC Dams)

    The client is required to approach AREX and obtain a form; visit the Rural District Council (RDC) for stamping before they come to ZimParks.

    The same form they bring to ZimParks and a payment of US$50.00 is paid as permit fee. The clients are requested to attach their company documents or corporate constitution and names of the co-operative members and submit to ZimParks Corporate Centre for processing.

  4. Commercial fishing in State Dams

    These include fishing in dams like Lake Chivero Dam; Darwendale Dam and Lake Kariba but these are fully subscribed.

    NB: Tugwi Mukosi is not yet fully matured to be considered for commercial fishing.

    Requirements

    • Formal application letter from the client to ZimParks Regional Office.
    • Assessment of the water body should be conducted by relevant authorities. Upon assessment they should submit a recommendation to the relevant authorities.
    • They should also attach valid permits, for example, to keep wildlife, wildlife ranching, breeders.
    • A permit fee of US$1000.00 is paid by the client per calendar year.

  5. Crayfish commercial permit

    • The client is required to submit a formal application letter. In their application letter the client is supposed to state the name of the dam that they intend to conduct their fishing from.
    • A payment fee of US$100.00 per 50 cages is paid by the client per month. These cages have specifications.

  6. Fish net dealers permit

Hunting

  1. Hunting quotas

    A quota is issued to a hunter, concessionaire leasing land to the land holder. A quota has set number of animals to be hunted from a certain concession or land within a space of a calendar yaer. When harvesting one is not supposed to exceed the limit given on the quota.

  2. Primary hunting permits

    A hunting permit is issued to a hunter, his or her agent, a concessionaire leasing land or the land holder. The hunting permit shows the name of the hunter or the property to be hunted, the animals to be hunted; their populations; sex and the amount paid for the hunting permit. The hunting permit is valid for a calendar year only. Animals shot; killed or wounded are considered harvested therefore they should be subtracted from the hunting permit. Animals not hunted by the last day of the validity of the permit cannot accumulate to the period following.

    NB: You shall not exceed to harvest the population of species stated on the hunting permit or the species not included on the hunting permit.

  3. Secondary hunting permits

    • Special hunting permits

      This is a notification to hunt which the Safari Operator in a specific area seeks from Zimparks. It involves using a sport hunting with a rifle. These are usually sought on species such as lions, leopards, and elephants. No payment is needed for these.

    • Bow and arrow permits

      Payment of US$1 500.00 is required to hunt on each property as requested by the Safari Operator.

    • Hounds permits

      This is hunting wildlife using dogs. A dog registration receipt of US$200 is paid annually if you are to hunt using dogs. Payment of US$1 500.00 is required to hunt on each property as requested by the Safari Operator.
